I'm the person in my family who handles everything — clothes for my husband, things for my baby, gifts for friends, and finds for myself. Shopping isn't just something I do. It's how I show love, solve problems, and balance indulgence with strategy.

I've spent almost five years at Walmart, most of that at Data Ventures building insights intelligence products from the ground up. What I keep returning to is how deeply I believe in what Walmart stands for: helping people save money, making smart choices accessible, and doing it the right way.

I'm energized by the ambiguity of building in AI. I find it exciting — not because of the novelty, but because the design problems are genuinely hard and the stakes are real.

"On nights when I have a lot of creative energy, I put on low-key 2000s music, write down every thought, then piece it together through sketching. That's how I make sense of complexity."
